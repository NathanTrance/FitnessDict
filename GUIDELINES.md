# Fitness Guidelines - nhatth2

Training philosophy: do the minimum that produces the greatest benefit. When life is busy, protect the habit and the big lifts first.

## Maintenance vs. progress
- Muscles start to atrophy after about **two weeks** without training. Hitting each body part **once per week** is usually enough to maintain size.
- Muscle loss is not dramatic, and it is reversible quickly.
- To retain muscle, you only need roughly **a third** of the volume/intensity required to build it.
- Strength depends on both muscle tissue **and** the nervous system, so practicing the main lifts regularly helps when possible.
- Cardiovascular fitness starts to decline after about **five days** of complete inactivity. Strength declines more slowly, often only after **two to three weeks**.

## Minimum effective dose
- If you can only train once per week, do one hard full-body session focused on big compound movements: squat, hinge, push, pull, carry/lunge.
- 1–2 hard sets per exercise, taken close to technical failure, are often enough to maintain (and sometimes build) strength.
- One high-quality session beats three rushed or low-effort sessions.

## Recovery and lifestyle
- Recovery is part of training. Sleep, protein intake, and stress management matter more than extra volume when time is limited.
- Consistency beats intensity over time. A short, easy session preserves the habit and prevents you from falling off.

## Autoregulation
- If you feel strong, push close to failure and add a little weight or volume.
- If you are stressed, tired, or short on time, cut the volume in half but keep the movements and the effort honest.
- Never grind a rep with bad form. Stop the set when form starts to break.
- Back-to-Back Hard Days: You can successfully string together two days of high-effort training because peak muscular discomfort typically hits 48 hours after the initial stress, rather than 24 hours.
## Training styles for running
- Interval (I) training on bikes (good for aerobic thresholds)
- Threshold (T) training on treadmills
- Easy runs (E) at home training

## Other training tips
- 30 minutes is the bare minimum for triggering baseline adaptations (increased capillary density and fat oxidation)
- 45-60 minutes is the sweet spot.
- over one hour for joints and muscle endurance development.
